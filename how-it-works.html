Trust & safety

Why neither side
can bypass the platform.

Five interlocking mechanisms make staying on NeedBridge more valuable than leaving it.

🎭
Proposal anonymity
Seekers see solution pitches — not solver identities. Identities are revealed only after a formal meeting request is approved on-platform. Seekers can't find solvers externally because they don't know who they are.
→ The introduction happens on NeedBridge
📋
Meeting records & 24-month clause
Every meeting is logged — date, time, participants. This creates legal proof of the introduction. The membership agreement includes a 24-month clause: any commercial deal with a NeedBridge-sourced contact is covered.
→ Bypassing creates legal risk
🔒
Pilot escrow
Pilot fees are held in platform escrow. Released automatically when the solver delivers. If delivery fails, the fee returns to the Seeker. Neither side takes payment risk.
→ Deliver first, payment follows
⏱️
90-day action rule
After a budget discussion starts, automated alerts go out at 30, 60, and 90 days. At day 90 without action, the listing suspends and the Seeker's commitment deposit is forfeited.
→ Dead listings cost money
⭐
Solver reputation lock
Verified badge, reference score, and priority matching are platform-exclusive. A solver who closes a deal off-platform permanently loses this reputation — and the compounding advantage it brings to future listings.
→ Long-term cost of leaving
🔍
Anomaly detection
The system flags unusual patterns: a Seeker viewing a profile repeatedly without acting, or a Solver later listing that Seeker as a client after marking an application "unsuccessful". Commission claims are triggered automatically.
→ Bypassing is harder than staying

Common questions

Is NeedBridge free to use?
+
Yes — fully free until October 2026 for everyone. After that, Seekers pay nothing (posting problems and reviewing proposals is always free). Solvers have a free Explorer tier with 3 applications/month. Unlimited applications start at €20/month on the Starter plan.
How is my company identity protected?
+
When you post a problem as a Seeker, your company name, logo, and contact information are never shown to Solvers. They see your sector, problem description, and budget — but not who you are. Your identity is revealed only when you formally approve a meeting request for a specific Solver.
What is the commitment deposit?
+
An optional deposit (€500–€2,000) that Seekers can place when posting a problem. If they complete at least 2 meetings with applicants within 90 days, it's fully refunded. If not, NeedBridge retains it — protecting Solvers from investing time in dead listings.
How does escrow work for pilots?
+
When a Seeker and Solver agree on a pilot, the Seeker deposits the agreed pilot fee into NeedBridge escrow. When the Solver delivers the agreed deliverable, the Seeker confirms and the escrow is released automatically. If the Solver fails to deliver, the fee is returned to the Seeker.
What is the success commission?
+
When a deal closes, a 1.5% success commission is charged to the Solver (not the Seeker). Growth plan subscribers pay 1%. For very small deals, a minimum flat fee of €1,500 applies. This only applies to deals sourced through NeedBridge and covered by your membership agreement.
Can I share a problem listing on LinkedIn?
+
Yes. Seekers can share any of their problem listings to LinkedIn with one click. The share posts the problem category, budget range, and a link to the listing — without revealing the company name. This helps Solvers in your network discover the opportunity.
